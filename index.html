EXPERIENCE KERALA



KASARGOD

The land of gods. The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasargod is famous for its handlooms and its 293 kilometre long coastline. Rich in history.

KANNUR

The City of Looms and Lores. known for the folk art called Theyyam. A artform encompassing dance, music and mime, where the artist does the guise of God.

KOZHIKODE

It was the Capital of Malabar during the Zamorin rule 500 years ago and is famous for its centuries-old trade in cotton and spices with Jews, Arabs,and Chinese.

WAYANAD

Adorning the northern hills of Kerala is the beautiful district of Wayanad, People are especially delighted by the range of exotic products including available here.

MALAPPURAM

Bounded by the Nilgiris hills on the east and the Arabian Sea on the west ,Malappuram has made numerous contributions to the cultural heritage of Kerala. A famous centre for spectacular festivals .

EXPLORE
PALAKKAD

Palakkad is also known as the rice bowl of Kerala. The 18th-century Palakkad Fort has sturdy battlements, a moat, and a Hanuman temple on its grounds.

EXPLORE
THRISSUR

Thrissur is known as the cultural capital of Kerala, and the land of Poorams. The district is known for its ancient temples, churches, and mosques.

EXPLORE
ERNAKULAM

The flourishing city of Ernakulam , in the South West Coast of Kerala, is renowned for its breath-taking beauty overlooking the Arabian Sea with backwater hubs.

EXPLORE
KOTTYAM

Flanked by the Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west, Kottayam is a place that is known for extraordinary qualities.

EXPLORE
IDUKKI

Known its rich flora and fauna. Home to the popular Eravikulam National Park and is characterized by the Anamudi mountain. Idukki is one of the greenest districts of Kerala.

EXPLORE
ALAPPUZHA

A town with canals, backwaters, beaches, and lagoons, Alappuzha was described by George Curzon, Viceroy of India at the start of the 20th century, as the "Venice of the East."

EXPLORE
KOLLAM

Kollam is one of the ancient civilizations in India and the oldest port cities in the Malabar Coast and was the capital city of historic Venad and Travancore Kingdom.

EXPLORE
PATHANAMTHITTA

The Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism, of the Kerala . Situated near the Western Ghats and bordered by the hills,its a real treat to eyes.

EXPLORE
THIRUVANANTHAPURAM

The 'Evergreen city of India', Thiruvananthapuram is the capital of the state of Kerala. The city comprises beautiful beaches, long stretches of palm fringed shorelines, windy backwaters and historical structures.
